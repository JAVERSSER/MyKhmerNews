Use 4 tricks to help your iPhone almost full of memory to revive

Every person's daily needs are many, such as storing large files, photos, videos, etc., which causes your iPhone to quickly fill up its memory. Therefore, Sabay will share a few quick tips to solve the problem of quickly filling up its memory in the following ways:

1. Offload Unused Apps is a feature that automatically deletes apps you don't use often, but it will keep the data in those apps. To enable this feature, go to Settings > General > iPhone Storage > Offload Unused Apps, then tap Enable.

2. Use Optimize iPhone Storage to compress photos/videos on your iPhone to a smaller size, but the original large photos will be stored in iCloud instead. To enable this feature, you need to enable iCloud Photos to upload photos or videos to iCloud first. To enable both features, go to Settings > Photos > iCloud Photos, then tap Enable, and then select the Optimize iPhone Storage check box.

3. Check your iPhone Storage. Because your phone has many apps, they often have a lot of data that requires memory, which can cause your memory to fill up without you realizing it. To check which apps are taking up the most space or memory, go to Settings > General > iPhone Storage. You will then see which apps are using the most memory, so you can go directly to that app to delete any unnecessary files.

4. Delete duplicate photos or videos in the Photos app. Your photos can sometimes have many overlapping or similar photos, so deleting some photos or videos can free up more space on your iPhone.
