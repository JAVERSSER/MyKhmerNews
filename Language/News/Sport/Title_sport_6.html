Host Cambodia ranks second in the Southeast Asian Open Water Swimming Championship as of November 3, 2024.

The first Southeast Asian Open Water Swimming Championships hosted by Cambodia have been underway for two days, with the hosts finishing second after winning two gold medals.

Mr. Hem Kiri, Secretary General of the Cambodian Swimming Federation, said at the opening ceremony of the recent competition that there are a total of 7 participating countries: host Cambodia, Vietnam, Thailand, Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia and the Philippines, and a total of 62 male and female athletes.

The swimming expert added that before the competition was organized, the member countries saw Cambodiaâ€™s progress and were confident after successfully hosting major events such as the SEA Games. Looking back at the two-day competition, the country ranked first was Vietnam after winning 9 gold medals, 9 silver medals, and 2 bronze medals, for a total of 20 medals. Cambodia came in second after winning 2 gold medals, 2 silver medals, and 3 bronze medals, for a total of 7 medals. Indonesia came in third with 1 gold medal, 1 silver medal, and 1 bronze medal, for a total of 3 medals. It is also worth noting that the first Southeast Asian Open Water Swimming Championship was held in Cambodia, with 7 countries participating. The competition was held from November 1 to 5, 2024, using the swimming pool of the National Stadium.
